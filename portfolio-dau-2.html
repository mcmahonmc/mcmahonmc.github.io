Secondhand Superstars

Charting Mercari's Course for Increased Engagement

Overview

Mercari’s mission is to create an easy and enjoyable platform for buying and selling secondhand goods, contributing to a more sustainable shopping ecosystem. The Secondhand Superstars project was developed to deepen user engagement by enhancing the experience for both buyers and sellers on the platform. By addressing key user pain points and motivations, we aimed to empower more people to participate in the circular economy and improve overall user satisfaction.

My Role

I led the research efforts for this project, focusing on understanding the behaviors, attitudes, and barriers that influenced user interactions with the platform. My responsibilities included conducting user interviews, designing and implementing surveys, analyzing platform data, and collaborating with cross-functional teams to turn insights into actionable improvements.

The Challenge

The secondhand market offers a great opportunity for sustainable shopping, but many users hesitate to engage due to concerns about the trustworthiness of sellers, the quality of items, and the overall complexity of the buying and selling process. Through this study, we identified the following primary challenges to address:

  • Building trust between buyers and sellers.
  • Simplifying the selling process to encourage more participation, especially for first-time sellers.
  • Improving buyer confidence in the quality of pre-owned items.

Approach

User Interviews & Surveys

We conducted in-depth interviews with both active and inactive users to understand their motivations, frustrations, and experiences with the platform. We also deployed targeted surveys to gather broader insights on what would drive more engagement in secondhand buying and selling. These methods helped us uncover key pain points, such as a lack of trust in the marketplace and difficulty navigating the listing process.

Data Analysis

Using data from user interactions, we identified drop-off points in the buyer and seller journeys. This allowed us to pinpoint where users were getting stuck or abandoning the process. By combining qualitative feedback with quantitative data, we developed a holistic view of the barriers users faced.

Co-Creation & Ideation Sessions

We held collaborative workshops with stakeholders from product, design, and engineering teams to brainstorm solutions based on our research findings. The goal was to create features that addressed user concerns while enhancing the overall platform experience. Several ideas emerged, including improved seller profiles, buyer guarantees, and streamlined item listing workflows.

New Product Roadmap

We redesigned the listing experience to accommodate casual sellers through the introduction of "lite listing," revamped our ratings system informed by a new wave of user research, and kicked off a new communities initiative to help people connect through commerce.

Reflection

This project reaffirmed the importance of understanding user motivations and pain points to drive meaningful improvements. By addressing concerns around trust and usability, we were able to create a more engaging and sustainable shopping experience. Working collaboratively across teams allowed us to implement solutions that were both user-centric and impactful, driving real business results for Mercari.
