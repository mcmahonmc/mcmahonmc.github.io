Student Profile
PsyBrain Lab

I am a fourth year doctoral candidate in the Department of Psychology at the University of Texas at Austin under the direction of Dr. David Schnyer. In my research, I use wearable electronics and neuroimaging to understand how daily patterns of sleep and activity are related to the neural network interactions underlying age-related changes in memory. I am a member of the Psychology Graduate Student Association leadership team and the Texas Aging and Longevity Center Graduate Student Council.

Before coming to UT, I worked at Virginia Commonwealth University where I had the opportunity to work on a variety of research projects related to traumatic brain injury, cognitive outcomes in cancer patients, disability and stigma, and brain computer interfaces. I also served as Director of Operations for Virginia Catalyst's funding initiative, which awards grants to collaborative project teams of university and industry leaders seeking to commercialize translational research innovations that improve human health.

In my free time, I love cuddling with my dog, Jack, and quilting!
