Summary

Mercari was on the cusp of developing three bold, new features. We needed a way to systematically monitor changes in ease of use, navigation, and core functionality over time.

My Role

I conceptualized and developed a systematic benchmarking survey program to measure how our product aligned with company objectives and UX values over time, including ease of use, navigation, competitor engagement, & product performance.

Tools

  • Looker
  • Live Intercept Survey
  • Statistical Analysis
What we built

Dashboard Overview

The UX Benchmarking dashboard allowed our research team to streamline analysis and reporting of rating scale, multiple choice, and open text survey responses collected from app users on a quarterly basis.
  • Accessible across full organization
  • Interactive filters for growth segments and generation
  • Joined survey data to behavioral analytics
  • Organized by content with descriptive fields

What we learned

Key Takeaways

Using AI generated themes from open-text responses, aided with manual inspection, three main opportunity areas emerged, including search (especially low-hit search queries and clothing queries), item discovery, and listing efficiency.
What I brought

Research Impact

  • Immediately influenced the product roadmap

    These findings were influential in approving a new focus on search UX improvements for the following quarter, and were immediately actioned on by our machine learning and customer relations management teams.

  • Upskilled our team in behavioral analytics

    Advocating for a Looker integration provided our team with greater need and opportunities to develop more quantitative skills. I led “Looker labs” and hosted office hours to support teammates in growing this skillset.

  • Built bridges with data engineering and business insights

    Quantitative data historically has been more accessible to Mercari employees. But in collaborating with new teams to build this integration, and in establishing our first company-facing dashboard, this opened more opportunities for user research consumption across the organization.
