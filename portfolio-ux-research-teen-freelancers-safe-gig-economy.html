Designing a Safe Freelancing Platform for Teens

Overview

Fiverr sought to expand its user base by exploring how to strategically position its platform to attract teens. My research focused on understanding parents' concerns about their children freelancing online and how to address these concerns through product features and positioning strategies.

The Challenge

With 32 million teens in the US lacking access to a dedicated freelancing platform, Fiverr saw an opportunity to expand into this demographic. However, launching a teen-focused platform presented challenges, including legal barriers and the need for parental approval. My goal was to explore how Fiverr could enable teens to freelance safely while addressing parentsâ€™ concerns.

Approach

I led a research effort to explore how parents view teens freelancing online, their concerns about safety and financial responsibility, and what assurances would make them comfortable with their teens using Fiverr. This included a combination of desk research, interviews, and thematic analysis to uncover key insights.

Key Decisions

  • Study Design. Working closely with the project team, I crafted a study plan that centered on understanding parents' levels of involvement in their teens' online activities, their concerns, and the assurances they needed. I also incorporated a design element, creating a mock email that parents could react to during the study.
  • Recruitment & Interviews. developed a 5-question screener and sourced participants through UserZoom, focusing on parents of teens aged 13-17 who had varying levels of familiarity with freelancing platforms. Eight parents participated in the study, and I used a sorting activity during interviews to help clarify default permissions for teen accounts.
  • Thematic Analysis & Reporting. After conducting the interviews, I used Reduct to tag and code transcripts, then affinity mapped the data to identify key themes. I summarized the findings in a report and presented them using video clips of parents sharing their thoughts directly, creating an engaging and human-centered presentation for stakeholders.

Findings

  • Parental Concerns. Parents expressed the need for strict moderation, verification of users, and supervision tools to ensure their teensâ€™ safety. Financial responsibility and online safety were paramount concerns.
  • Potential Adoption Pathways. The study revealed that creating teen accounts as sub-accounts under a parentâ€™s profile might not be a barrier to adoption, as parents saw this as a way to maintain control while their teens gained freelancing experience.
  • Fiverr as a Learning Platform. Parents viewed Fiverr as a valuable platform for teens to gain business skills under parental guidance, opening opportunities for future product features that emphasize learning.

Reflection & Learnings

One challenge was legal, as Fiverr had historically restricted data collection for minors. The research moderation tool was also nearing a contractual deadline, limiting sampling capabilities. However, through creative recruitment and careful planning, I ensured that the study was completed with high-quality data that delivered actionable insights.

Outcome

The findings from this study led Fiverrâ€™s leadership to reassess the resources needed to develop a teen freelancing platform. While the direction was ultimately deprioritized due to conflicts with other business goals, the research generated 17 actionable recommendations for future product and marketing development, should this direction be revisited.
