Summary

We built a listing import tool that leverages LLM to generate metadata and key search terms from a cross-posted item URL. My research identified how to best use the LLM, improved the user interface, and guided post-launch feature prioritization.

What we built

Feature Overview

With URL listing, power sellers can expand their audience with ease.
  • Cross-post to Mercari from eBay or Depop
  • Import one-at-a-time, or all at once
  • Flexible shipping options
  • Review listings before going live
Where I fit in

Research Process


Study Plan

Before diving into ideation and usability testing, I wanted to validate our current understanding of our existing Mercari power sellers' unmet needs. With that understanding, we could move forward with design, which I then wanted to test with our target audience of eBay power sellers. Finally, I planned a post-launch survey to quickly identify and prioritize new opportunities for iteration to optimize feature performance.

For each phase of the study plan, I sought participation and feedback from design, content, and product management. Together, we refined key research questions, study design, and discussion guides to ensure the data gathered would support decision-making.

What we learned

Key Takeaways

For existing sellers with established workflows, its value depends on the number of platforms they use and their perception of the new marketplace, including fees, speed of sale, and ease of shipping. We also learned that sellers did not want to use the LLM for certain fields, but rather preferred to use their own personalized listing conventions. We identified opportunties to improve the user flow, bulk editing capabilities, and visual progress bar. Finally, addressing concerns about accuracy and authenticity was crucial for feature adoption.
What I brought

Research Impact

  • Changed order of account creation in the user flow

    We thought it would save new listers time by creating their account while waiting for their listings to import. Because sellers didn't yet have trust in the platform, this came across as untrustworthy.

  • Emphasized zero selling fees on the landing page fees

    Sellers weren't bought into the idea of listing on a new platform just because it was easy - they first needed to understand the fee policy and shipping options.

  • Made progress visual

    Text wasn't effective enough at conveying progress - visuals helped build confidence in the feature.

  • Improved error transparency and guidance

    Early adopters reported that they didn't know how to correct their actions when an import didn't work, so we linked the FAQ page directly on the import listing page.
